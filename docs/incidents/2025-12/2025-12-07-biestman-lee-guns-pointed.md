# Elderly Couple Has Guns Pointed at Them After Following ICE Vehicle

## Summary
John Biestman and Janet Lee, a married couple in their late 60s from Linden Hills, had guns pointed at them and were threatened with arrest after following an ICE vehicle from a raid at Church of the Assumption of the Blessed Mary in Richfield.

## Sources
1. Minnesota Reformer (Dec 17, 2025): [ACLU sues ICE, alleging agency violates constitutional rights of observers and protesters](https://minnesotareformer.com/2025/12/17/aclu-sues-ice-alleging-agency-violates-constitutional-rights-of-observers-and-protesters/)
2. ACLU-MN (Dec 17, 2025): [ACLU-MN, Pro Bono Partners Sue to Protect Protesters, Observers and Journalists](https://www.aclu-mn.org/press-releases/1a/)

## Victim(s)
- **Names:** John Biestman and Janet Lee
- **Ages:** Late 60s
- **Citizenship:** U.S. Citizens
- **Residence:** Linden Hills, Minneapolis

## Timeline
- **~11:30 AM** - ICE vehicles observed circling Church of the Assumption in Richfield
- **Raid** - ICE conducts operation at the church
- **Following** - Biestman and Lee follow ICE vehicle after raid
- **Confrontation** - ICE agents box in the couple's car
- **Threat** - Agents point guns at elderly couple and threaten to arrest them

## Legal Action
Biestman and Lee are named plaintiffs in ACLU of Minnesota lawsuit challenging ICE's treatment of observers and protesters.

## Context
The incident occurred at a Catholic church, one of the sensitive locations where faith leaders across Minneapolis-St. Paul organized to protect Somali communities from ICE operations.

**HIGH** - Named plaintiffs in ACLU federal lawsuit with specific allegations in legal complaint. Church raid at sensitive location. Documented pattern of threatening observers.
