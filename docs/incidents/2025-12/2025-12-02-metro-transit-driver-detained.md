# Somali-American Metro Transit Bus Driver Detained Over a Month

## Summary
A Somali-American Metro Transit bus operator was detained by ICE on December 2, 2025, while on his way to work. He was held in ICE detention facilities in Iowa and Nebraska for over a month before being released without explanation on January 6, 2026. The Amalgamated Transit Union Local 1005 called the detention "unlawful."

## Timeline
- **December 2, 2025 (morning)** - Bus operator arrested by ICE while commuting to work
- **December 2025** - Transferred to ICE detention facility in Iowa
- **Late December 2025** - Transferred again to Nebraska detention facility
- **January 6, 2026** - Released without explanation, reunited with family
- **January 14, 2026** - Union President David Stiggers speaks at press conference about case

## Detention Details
The driver was held for over a month across multiple states:
- Initially detained in the Minneapolis area
- Transferred to Iowa detention facility
- Further transferred to Nebraska detention facility
- Released January 6, 2026

## Official Accounts

### Union Statement
ATU Local 1005 President David Stiggers stated the detention was "unlawful" and condemned ICE activity as "a throwback to the darkest times of human history, 1940s Germany."

### ICE/DHS Statement
No official statement released regarding this specific detention.

## Union Response
At a January 14, 2026 press conference, Stiggers announced:
- Metro Transit workers calling on ICE not to interfere with transit operations
- Bus drivers have the right to refuse to open doors for masked, unidentified agents
- Union advises drivers that armed agents without identification pose a safety risk

## Context
This detention occurred at the start of "Operation Metro Surge," the largest immigration enforcement operation in American history according to DHS. The operation deployed over 2,000 ICE, Border Patrol, and other DHS agents to the Twin Cities.
