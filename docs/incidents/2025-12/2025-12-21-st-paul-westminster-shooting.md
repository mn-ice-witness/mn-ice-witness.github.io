# ICE Agent Fires Shots During St. Paul Arrest After Being Struck by Vehicle

## Summary
An ICE agent fired two shots during an arrest attempt in St. Paul after being struck by the suspect's vehicle. Two ICE officers were hospitalized with non-life-threatening injuries. No one was hit by gunfire. The suspect, a Cuban national, was apprehended after ramming multiple vehicles and attempting to flee on foot.

## Sources
1. Sahan Journal (Dec 21, 2025): [ICE agent fires shots during arrest in St. Paul after being struck with vehicle](https://sahanjournal.com/news-partners/ice-agent-fires-shots-during-arrest-st-paul/)

## Suspect
- **Nationality:** Cuban national (not a U.S. citizen per ICE)
- **Status:** In ICE custody pending charges

## Timeline
- **~8:20 AM** - St. Paul Police called to 1300 block of Westminster Street for reports of shots fired
- **Vehicle stop** - ICE attempts vehicle stop; suspect refuses to comply
- **First collision** - Suspect drives away, striking an ICE officer
- **Chase** - Suspect drives recklessly, hitting parked vehicles and ramming ICE vehicle
- **Second collision** - Suspect hits second ICE officer
- **Shots fired** - ICE agent "defensively fired" two rounds; no one struck
- **Flight** - Suspect drives to apartment complex, rams another ICE vehicle
- **Arrest** - Suspect attempts to flee on foot, apprehended after resisting arrest and biting an officer

## Injuries
- Two ICE officers hospitalized with non-life-threatening injuries
- Suspect hospitalized for evaluation

## Official Response
The incident will undergo review per agency policy, including independent review following initial investigation.

## Context
This occurred during "Operation Metro Surge." DHS reported arresting over 670 people in Minnesota that month.

## Editorial Assessment
**HIGH** - Reported by Sahan Journal with specific details from St. Paul Police. One of two ICE shooting incidents during Operation Metro Surge (the other being Renee Good's death).
