# U.S. Citizen Observer Detained 5 Hours, Wedding Ring Cut Off

## Summary
Sue Tincher, a 55-year-old Minneapolis resident and U.S. citizen, was detained for over five hours after responding to a neighborhood alert about ICE activity. Within 15 seconds of arriving to observe, she was knocked to the ground and handcuffed. Officers cut off her wedding ring at the detention facility.

## Sources

2. [ACLU sues ICE, alleging agency violates constitutional rights](https://minnesotareformer.com/2025/12/17/aclu-sues-ice-alleging-agency-violates-constitutional-rights-of-observers-and-protesters/) - Minnesota Reformer

## Victim(s)
- **Name:** Sue Tincher
- **Age:** 55
- **Citizenship:** U.S. Citizen
- **Residence:** Minneapolis

## Timeline
- **~6:20 AM** - Tincher responds to neighborhood alert about ICE activity on Oliver Avenue N
- **Arrival** - Federal agents tell her to move back
- **15 seconds later** - After refusing and questioning if they were ICE, officers "knocked me to the ground"
- **Detention** - Handcuffed and placed in van
- **Transport** - Taken to Bishop Henry Whipple Federal Building at Fort Snelling
- **Interrogation** - Read Miranda rights, interviewed; declined to answer without counsel
- **Shortly after noon** - Released after attorneys contacted the facility

## Injuries
- Wedding ring cut off by officers
- Hand bruising from tight handcuffing
- No serious injuries

## Victim Statement

### Sue Tincher
"There was no warning, no explanation, no chance to comply."

On the experience: Within 15 seconds of arriving, she was "on the ground in handcuffs" and was "put in shackles" at the facility.

## Official Response

### Governor Tim Walz
Sent letter to Homeland Security Secretary Kristi Noem demanding review of the arrest and calling the agents' conduct "unlawful."

## Legal Action
Tincher is a named plaintiff in ACLU of Minnesota lawsuit (Tincher v. Noem) challenging ICE violence and misconduct towards Minnesotans exercising First Amendment rights.
