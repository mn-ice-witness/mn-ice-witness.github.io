# ICE Using El Burrito Mercado as "Hunting Ground" - 47-Year-Old Business Under Siege

## Summary
Federal immigration agents have been circling El Burrito Mercado, a prominent 47-year-old Mexican supermarket and restaurant in St. Paul's West Side, for over a week. CEO Milissa Silva-Diaz stated "ICE is using my business as a hunting ground" as unmarked vans and masked agents have scared off customers and workers, forcing the business to close or limit hours. The business has implemented safety measures including posting guards at entrances.

## What's Happening
ICE agents have been observed:
- Circling the business in unmarked vans
- Parking and watching the parking lot
- "Swirling around the block waiting for people"
- Creating an atmosphere of intimidation

## Impact on Business
- **Monday:** Closed entirely
- **Wednesday:** Open only 4 hours
- Staff calling out sick due to fear
- Customers staying home
- Sales dropped dramatically (businesses across area reporting 50-80% declines)

## Safety Measures Implemented
- Parking lot reduced to single entrance
- Guards stationed at entrance checking for ICE
- Men posted at door opening for customers
- Time limits requested for shopping
