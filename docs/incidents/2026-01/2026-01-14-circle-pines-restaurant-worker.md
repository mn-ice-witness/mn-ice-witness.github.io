# Restaurant Worker with Legal Visa Detained by Plain-Clothed Agents

## Summary
A restaurant worker in Circle Pines with a valid legal work visa was detained by two plain-clothed federal agents lying in wait outside the business. The worker has no criminal record and fled to the U.S. under asylum protections after a cartel took over his family's factory in his home country.

- **Background:** Fled home country after cartel took over family's factory; family feared for their lives

## What Happened
Video footage shows the moment a restaurant worker was grabbed by federal agents outside the business. Two plain-clothed agents were lying in wait outside the restaurant and detained the worker as he exited.

## Current Status
The worker is being held at the federal Whipple Building in Minneapolis. The restaurant owner traveled to the facility to deliver personal items including his glasses.

## Employer Response
The restaurant owner confirmed:
- Employee has a valid work visa
- Employee has no criminal record in the U.S. or his native country
- Employee came to the U.S. under asylum protections
- Restaurant is working to help the employee

The owner still isn't certain what agency the agents were from or what specific reason was given for the arrest and detention.
