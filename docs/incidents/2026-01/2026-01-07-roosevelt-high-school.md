# Border Patrol Agents at Roosevelt High School, Staffer Detained

## Summary
Federal agents descended on Roosevelt High School during dismissal, tackled several people, and deployed chemical irritants. A special education assistant who is a U.S. citizen was detained and later released. Minneapolis Public Schools canceled classes for the rest of the week.

## How It Started
DHS claims Border Patrol was pursuing someone who rammed a government vehicle and led agents on a 5-mile chase before stopping near the school.

## Official Accounts

### DHS Statement
Claimed staffer "identified himself as a teacher" and "proceeded to assault a border patrol agent." Denied using tear gas.

### Local Officials
- **Minneapolis Public Schools:** Canceled classes Jan 8-9 "out of an abundance of caution"
- **Governor Walz:** Told ICE to "stay away" from Minnesota schools
- **Minneapolis Federation of Educators:** Stated tear gas was used

## Witness Accounts
- Witness Cara Morrow saw agents tackle people on school grounds
- KARE 11 witnesses said tear gas was used
- Teacher Joelle Stangler: staffer's "role in that moment would have been keeping students and his fellow staff safe"
- One witness: "They were filming this whole thing like it was a reality TV show"

## Conflicting Accounts
Significant dispute over: (1) whether tear gas was used; (2) whether staffer assaulted agent

## Aftermath
- Schools closed Jan 8-9
- Jesse Ventura (Roosevelt graduate) condemned actions
- State lawsuit filed
